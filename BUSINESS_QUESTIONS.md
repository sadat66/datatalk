# Business Questions (Northwind)


These are decision-oriented questions a business user would realistically ask

## What Counts As A Real Business Question

A strong question usually includes:

- A business decision goal
- A measurable metric (revenue, orders, units, freight, etc.)
- A slice or grain (by month, region, category, customer)
- A time window (specific quarter/year or sample period)

## Executive Snapshot

- What were total revenue and order count in 1997, and how did they change vs 1996?
- Which 3 categories contributed the most to revenue in 1997?
- Which sales regions underperformed in Q4 1997 compared with Q3 1997?
- How concentrated is revenue among the top 10 customers in 1997?

## Sales Performance

- Show monthly revenue trend for 1997; where are the biggest month-over-month drops?
- Which products had the highest revenue growth from 1996 to 1997?
- Which categories have high order count but low average line value?
- Which countries generated the most revenue per order in 1997?

## Customer Analysis

- Who are the top 10 customers by revenue in 1997, and what share of total do they represent?
- Which customers reduced order volume the most from 1996 to 1997?
- What is the split of revenue between repeat customers and one-time customers in 1997?
- Which customers place frequent orders but have low average order value?

## Operations And Fulfillment

- Which shippers have the highest average freight cost per order in 1997?
- Which regions have the highest late-shipment risk proxy (long ship lag from order date)?
- How does freight cost trend month by month in 1997?
- Which product categories are most associated with high freight cost orders?

## Pricing And Discount Impact

- By category, how does average discount relate to revenue in 1997?
- Which products had high discount rates but weak unit growth?
- What is revenue for minimum-discount vs high-discount orders in 1997?
- Which customers receive the highest average discount and what revenue do they drive?
